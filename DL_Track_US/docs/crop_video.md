In order to save time when analysing videos, you can cut out the frames without any movement at the start and/or end of videos.
To do this, follow these steps:

- Once you started the GUI and the main GUI window opened, click on the
<span style="color: #a34ba1;">**Advanced Methods**</span>.
- In the <span style="color: #2eaf66;">**Select Method**</span> Dropdown select “Crop Video”. The separate
“Crop Video Window” will pop up.

<img src="\md_graphics\crop_video\advanced_methods.png">
<img src="\md_graphics\crop_video\select_method.png" width ="120">
<img src="\md_graphics\crop_video\crop_video_window.png" width="200">

- First, you need to specify the video you want to crop. Click the <span style="color: #a34ba1;">**Load Video**</span> button and select the video.

<img src="\md_graphics\crop_video\load_video_button.png" width="200">

After successfully loading the video, the UI should look like this.

- With the yellow slider you can watch through the video and determine the sections without muscle movement.
- Then you can type in the <span style="color: #299ed9;">**Start- and End Frame**</span>.

<img src="\md_graphics\crop_video\select_frames.png" width ="400">

- By clicking the <span style="color: #a34ba1;">**Browse**</span> button you can selcect the folder, where the cropped video is saved.
- Finally, click <span style="color: #2eaf66;">**Crop Video**</span>.

<img src="\md_graphics\crop_video\select_output.png" width ="400">
